This page brings together high-quality resources for adults with ADHD and their families. It is intended as a starting point — not a substitute for advice from your own medical team.

---

## ADHD organisations

**In Canada**

- [CADDAC — Centre for ADHD Awareness, Canada](https://caddac.ca/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — Canada's national ADHD charity, offering education, advocacy, virtual adult support groups, and group coaching programmes for adults living with ADHD.
- [CADDRA — Canadian ADHD Resource Alliance](https://www.caddra.ca/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — A national alliance of health professionals advancing ADHD care in Canada. Their public section includes patient guides, questionnaires, and information on accessing ADHD assessment across Canadian provinces.

**International**

- [CHADD — Children and Adults with ADHD](https://chadd.org/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — One of the most comprehensive ADHD resources available. CHADD provides science-based information, online education modules, local support chapters, and a national helpline. Note: CHADD Vancouver runs a monthly in-person support group for adults in the Vancouver area.

## Understanding ADHD in adults

- [I'm an adult and I think I might have ADHD](https://www.heretohelp.bc.ca/q-and-a/im-an-adult-and-i-think-i-might-have-adhd){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — Here to Help BC guide to the steps for getting assessed in British Columbia, including where to start and what to expect.
- [Diagnosis of ADHD in adults](https://chadd.org/for-adults/diagnosis-of-adhd-in-adults/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — CHADD's overview of how adult ADHD is diagnosed, including assessment criteria and what a thorough evaluation involves.
- [Adult ADHD — symptoms and causes](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/adult-adhd/symptoms-causes/syc-20350878){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — Mayo Clinic explanation of how ADHD presents in adults, why it often goes unrecognised, and what conditions can co-occur.
- [ADHD in adults](https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/adhd-adults/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — NHS overview of adult ADHD including symptoms, causes, diagnosis, and treatment.

## Living with ADHD

- [Managing adult ADHD](https://www.helpguide.org/mental-health/adhd/managing-adult-adhd){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — HelpGuide's evidence-based practical guide to managing ADHD in daily life, including work, relationships, time management, and sleep.
- [CADDAC programmes and events](https://caddac.ca/programs-and-events/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — CADDAC's calendar of virtual adult support groups, group coaching sessions, and webinars covering executive function, relationships, and daily living with ADHD.
- [For adults — CHADD](https://chadd.org/for-adults/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — CHADD's adult hub covering employment, relationships, managing finances, parenting with ADHD, and other life domains.

## Mental health support in BC

Adults with ADHD have higher rates of anxiety and depression. Support is available.

- [BounceBack BC](https://bouncebackbc.ca/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — A free, evidence-based cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) programme run by CMHA BC for adults and youth 13+ managing low mood, anxiety, or stress. Delivered online or by phone with a coach.
- [Here to Help BC](https://www.heretohelp.bc.ca/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — BC-based mental health information, self-assessments, and connections to local services.
- [Canadian Mental Health Association — BC](https://bc.cmha.ca/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — Find mental health programmes and peer support in your community across BC.
- [Crisis Line Association of BC](https://www.crisislines.bc.ca/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — Directory of volunteer-operated crisis and distress lines across British Columbia.
- **310 Mental Health Line** — Call or text **310-6789** (no area code needed) for free, 24/7 emotional support and mental health referrals anywhere in BC.
- **National Crisis Line** — Call or text **9-8-8** (toll-free, 24/7) for immediate support if you are in crisis.

## Attention and executive function strategies

ADHD affects not just attention but a broad set of skills known as executive functions — planning, organisation, working memory, and emotional regulation. These resources offer evidence-based strategies.

- [CADDAC adult coaching and support](https://caddac.ca/programs-and-events/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — CADDAC's virtual group coaching programmes focus specifically on building executive function skills for adults with ADHD.
- [Managing adult ADHD — beyond medication](https://www.helpguide.org/mental-health/adhd/managing-adult-adhd){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — HelpGuide's guide to non-medication strategies including behavioural approaches, structure, exercise, sleep, and mindfulness.
- [Adult ADHD — diagnosis and treatment](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/adult-adhd/diagnosis-treatment/drc-20350883){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} — Mayo Clinic overview of ADHD treatment options including therapy, skills training, and lifestyle strategies alongside medication.
