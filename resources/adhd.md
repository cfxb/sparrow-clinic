## What is ADHD?

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterised by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning. While ADHD is often identified in childhood, it frequently persists into adulthood and can go undiagnosed for many years.

Adults with ADHD may experience difficulties with sustained attention, organisation, time management, and emotional regulation. These challenges can affect work performance, relationships, and quality of life.

## Why see a neuropsychologist?

A neuropsychological assessment provides an objective, detailed picture of cognitive strengths and weaknesses. This goes beyond rating scales or checklists to examine the specific cognitive processes underlying attention, working memory, processing speed, and executive function.

This level of detail is valuable when:

- A diagnosis is uncertain or has not been established
- Symptoms overlap with other conditions such as anxiety, depression, or learning disabilities
- There are academic or workplace accommodation needs requiring documentation
- Prior treatments have not been effective and a clearer picture of cognitive profile is needed

## What does an assessment involve?

An ADHD assessment at Sparrow Neuropsychology includes a clinical interview, standardised cognitive testing, and a review of relevant background information. Testing typically takes three to four hours and covers attention, processing speed, working memory, and executive function.

A detailed written report follows, summarising findings, diagnosis where applicable, and specific recommendations for treatment, accommodations, and support strategies.
